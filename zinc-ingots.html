Zinc Ingots

Zinc ingots are solid blocks of refined zinc metal, produced through smelting and refining processes. They serve as raw materials for various industrial applications, such as galvanization, alloy production, and die casting. Zinc’s corrosion resistance, malleability, and ability to form alloys make it indispensable in many industries.

FEATURES

Corrosion Resistance

Zinc is an excellent corrosion-resistant material used for galvanizing.

Malleability

Zinc is easily shaped and formed, making it ideal for various manufacturing processes.

Low Melting Point

Zinc's low melting point makes it energy-efficient for processing and casting.

Electrical Conductivity

Zinc is a good conductor of electricity, useful in batteries, fittings, and electronics.

Eco-Friendly

Zinc is recyclable and biodegradable, making it an environmentally friendly material.

APPLICATIONS

Zinc is used to galvanize metals, to prevent corrosion significantly extend the lifespan of steel structures and components.

Galvanizing

Zinc alloys are widely used in die casting to create metal components across many industries, including automotive, electronics, and hardware.

Die Casting

Zinc is a key ingredient in various alloys, such as brass and bronze that are used in a wide range of applications, from plumbing fixtures to musical instruments.

Alloy Production

Zinc compounds find applications in various chemical processes. They are used in making paints, pigments, rubber, and ceramics.

Chemical Applications

Zinc is used in manufacturing of zinc-based batteries, such as alkaline batteries. These are commonly used in various electronic devices due to their reliability and cost-effectiveness.

Battery Manufacturing

Zinc compounds are used in the construction industry to enhance material properties. They are used in cement, concrete, and other construction materials to improve their durability and performance.
